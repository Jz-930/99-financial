Why Consider an IFA?

An IFA allows business owners and incorporated professionals to retain liquidity while building long-term, tax-efficient wealth through a corporate-owned participating life insurance policy.

Instead of tying up large premium payments, you can borrow back most of the premium immediately via a bank line of credit secured against the policy’s cash value.

Key Advantages

  • Maintain Liquidity Keep capital working in your business.
  • Tax-Deductible Interest Potential deduction when funds are used for income generation.
  • Enhanced Growth Tax-sheltered growth inside the policy.
  • Estate Benefits Significant CDA credit at death.

How an IFA Works

A structured process to leverage your insurance policy.

01

Apply for Policy

Apply for a corporate-owned participating life insurance policy.

02

Pay Initial Premium

Upon approval, the corporation pays the initial premium.

03

Assign Collateral

The corporation assigns the policy as collateral to a lending institution.

04

Bank Advances Funds

Bank advances 75–100% of CSV, depending on lender and structure.

05

Service Interest

The corporation services interest payments on the outstanding loan.

06

Repeat Annually

Process is repeated as new premiums are paid and credit adjusted.

Who Is a Good Candidate?

  • High-income professionals with Professional Corporations.
  • Corporations with significant retained earnings seeking tax efficiency.
  • Families with estate, legacy, or intergenerational wealth objectives.

IFA + CDA Integration

At death, the corporation receives the life insurance death benefit. The credit to the Capital Dividend Account (CDA) is generally equal to the death benefit minus the policy’s adjusted cost basis (ACB). The outstanding bank loan is repaid from the death benefit, and the remaining amount, plus the CDA credit, can be distributed to shareholders tax-free.

Result: Loan Repaid | Tax-Free Distribution

Risks & Considerations

An IFA is a sophisticated strategy that requires a clear understanding of potential risks. We model multiple scenarios to ensure robustness.

Interest Rate Risk

Rising rates can increase the cost of carrying the loan and affect corporate cash flow.

Financing Risk

Lenders may adjust advance rates, collateral requirements, or covenants over time.

Cash Flow Dependence

Assumes stable corporate cash flow to comfortably service interest costs.

Long-Term Commitment

Cancelling prematurely can reduce effectiveness and create tax consequences.

Collateral Deficit / Margin Risk

Collateral requirements may change. If CSV grows slowly or lending criteria tightens, additional collateral may be required on short notice.

Our Approach: We stress-test each plan against multiple interest rate and collateral scenarios before implementation.
