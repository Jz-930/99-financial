What Is Participating Life Insurance?

Participating life insurance (“Par Insurance”) is a long-term asset class that combines guaranteed values, dividend growth potential, tax-efficient accumulation, and estate advantages. Par insurance provides lifelong coverage while building cash value through guaranteed growth and policyholder dividends.

A foundational asset for corporate wealth building and preservation.

Why Corporations Use Par Insurance

Tax-Efficient Growth

Funds grow tax-deferred, creating a stable asset uncorrelated to market volatility, allowing capital to compound more efficiently than passive investments.

Access to Cash Value

Access cash via withdrawals, policy loans, or as collateral for a line of credit, making it a versatile balance sheet asset for liquidity needs.

Tax-Free Estate Benefit

The death benefit creates a Capital Dividend Account (CDA) credit, allowing proceeds to be paid to shareholders 100% tax-free.

How Corporate-Owned Insurance Works

Follow the journey of a corporate dollar as it transforms into a tax-free legacy.

1

Funding & Growth

The corporation pays premiums with lower-taxed corporate dollars. The policy's cash value then grows, sheltered from annual taxation.

2

Lifetime & Death

During lifetime, the policy can be collateralized for liquidity. Upon passing, the tax-free death benefit flows into the corporation.

3

Distribution

The death benefit (less ACB) creates a large CDA credit, allowing a 100% tax-free distribution to the shareholder’s family or estate.

Par Insurance & Tax-Free Retirement

A corporate-owned par policy can be used to generate tax-free retirement income, creating a sophisticated alternative to traditional taxable retirement plans.

1. Funding

Premiums are funded using retained earnings; cash value compounds tax-deferred.

2. Retirement Cash Flow

Access this value through policy or bank collateral loans, creating tax-free cash flow while preserving RRSP/TFSA room.

3. Estate Settlement

At death, tax-free proceeds repay loans and credit the CDA, allowing tax-free distributions to heirs.
