Strategy: IFA Leverage

Leveraging Corporate Success

45-year-old business owner wants to implement a long-term insurance and estate strategy while keeping corporate liquidity available for investment opportunities.

How It Works

  • Corporate Par Policy: $100,000 annual premium for 20 years ($2.6M initial coverage).
  • Collateral Loan: Corporation borrows back 100% of premium at Prime rate.
  • Deductibility: Interest payments may be tax-deductible if funds are used for business investment.

Medical Professional
Case Study 3

Professional Corporation Strategy

Physician earning $600k+ annually. Low corporate tax allows retained earnings to build up, but RRSP limits are too small relative to income.

RRSP vs. Corporate Par

RRSP: Withdrawals are fully taxable (up to 53%). Subject to market volatility.

Corporate Par: Grows tax-deferred. Access via loans is tax-free. Death benefit creates CDA.

The Result

  • Predictable, tax-efficient growth inside PC.
  • Reduced dependence on taxable RRSP.
  • Liquidity to fund estate taxes without selling assets.
Generation Transfer

Passing the Torch

Estate Freeze + Corporate Par + CDA

Parents want to transition a $4M business to their son, maintain control during their lifetime, and ensure tax liability doesn't cripple the company.

The Strategy

  1. Estate Freeze: Lock in current value for parents ($4M preferred shares). Future growth passes to son.
  2. Insurance Funding: Corporate policy funds the tax liability on the frozen shares at death.

Outcome at Age 85

Death Benefit $4.49M
CDA Credit Created $4.25M
Tax-Free to Son
$4.25M

Result: Business remains intact; tax liability on frozen shares fully funded.

High-Income Lawyer (PC)

Tax Optimization & Trapped Retained Earnings

A lawyer earning $300k+ faces a 53% personal tax rate. Cash is trapped inside the PC. Passive investment income threatens the Small Business Deduction.

The Solution

The PC implements a corporate-owned Par policy ($30k-$45k annual premium). The PC is owner and beneficiary.

Key Wins

  • Converts trapped cash into a tax-sheltered asset.
  • Protects SBD: Reduces passive income exposure.
  • Retirement: Tax-free income via corporate loans.
  • Estate: Large CDA credit for tax-free payout.
