Family Business Transition

Estate Freeze & Succession

The Challenge

A family-owned business preparing to transition to the son (or next generation) requires careful, professional planning. Without a structured approach, significant tax exposure, liquidity shortages, or family conflicts may arise.

Combining an estate freeze with corporate-owned participating life insurance allows the family to lock in tax liabilities, shift growth to the next generation, and build a substantial tax-free legacy.

Key Pain Points

Unchecked Growth of Tax Liability

Without a freeze, the business continues to grow in value, increasing the tax burden upon death. This can create a liquidity crisis.

The Solution

The Estate Freeze + Insurance Solution

Locks in the current value of the business shares for the parents and funds the tax liability.

1

Estate Freeze

Locks in today's value for parents (Preferred Shares) and shifts future growth to the next generation (Common Shares).

2

Funds Tax Liability

Corporate Par Insurance creates tax-free death benefit to pay capital gains tax, probate fees, and legal costs.

3

Credits the CDA

The death benefit flows into the Capital Dividend Account, allowing the company to distribute money to the son tax-free.

Illustrative Outcome (Parents Age 85)

Family business valued at $4M. $100K annual premium for 10 years.

Paid to corporation

CDA Credit

~ $4.25M

To Son

Tax-free distribution

Summary of Value

Smooth, tax-efficient business transition Protection of the company’s long-term value Zero forced sale of assets Tax-free capital to support the next generation
