Why Estate & Succession Planning Matters

For many Canadian business owners and high-net-worth families, long-term wealth preservation is just as important as wealth creation. Effective estate and succession planning ensures a smooth transition of family assets, eliminates unnecessary tax exposure, and maximizes the value transferred to the next generation.

Family Business Succession

Transitioning a family business to the next generation requires careful planning. Key considerations include valuation, tax efficiency, and maintaining family harmony.

Corporate-owned participating whole life insurance supports this by:

  • Providing liquidity to fund buy-sell agreements.
  • Equalizing inheritance among children (especially when only one child continues the business).
  • Reducing reliance on business assets to settle taxes.
  • Ensuring the business remains intact and financially secure.

Estate Freeze Strategies

An estate freeze is a sophisticated tax-planning technique commonly used by entrepreneurs and shareholders of Canadian-controlled private corporations (CCPCs).

The Mechanism

It locks in the current value of the business for the owner (via Preferred Shares), while future growth is shifted to the next generation or a family trust (via Common Shares).

*This creates a known, fixed tax liability at death, but without cash, the estate may still struggle.

The Insurance Solution

  • Funds the tax liability created at death on frozen preferred shares.
  • Ensures the estate does not need to sell assets or borrow at an unfavourable time.
  • Provides tax-free capital flowing into the Capital Dividend Account (CDA).

  • Allowing parents to retain control of corporate capital while gradually shifting benefits.
  • Working alongside family trusts for flexible allocation.

Case Study: Family Business Transition

Real world application of Estate Freeze + Corporate Insurance

The Scenario

A family-owned business ($4M Value) is preparing to transition to the son. The parents (Age 60) want to pass on control while managing tax exposure and ensuring fairness.

The Strategy

By combining an Estate Freeze (locking in today’s value) with a Corporate-Owned Participating Life Policy, the family funds the tax liability at death and creates a large CDA credit for tax-free distributions.
