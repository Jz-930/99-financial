What Is the CDA?

The Capital Dividend Account (CDA) is a notional tax account used by the CRA that tracks amounts a corporation may distribute tax-free as a capital dividend. It is not a bank account — it is a tax mechanism that allows a corporation to distribute certain types of proceeds to shareholders entirely tax-free.

How CDA Helps Business Owners

A properly structured CDA strategy allows you to achieve significant financial advantages.

How Life Insurance Creates CDA Credit

When a corporation owns a participating life insurance policy, it creates a predictable and powerful CDA credit upon death.

1

Policy grows tax-sheltered inside the corporation.

2

Upon death, the death benefit is paid to the corporation.

3

The benefit minus the ACB is credited to the CDA.

4

The corporation pays this amount out tax-free.

Example Calculation

Corporate-owned policy death benefit $3,000,000
Policy ACB at death ($200,000)
CDA credit created $2,800,000

→ Entire $2.8M can be paid out to family tax-free.
